WHAT IS THIS EXPERTISE? WHY DOES IT MATTER?

Results are what we all want to achieve – positive measurable evidence that what we are doing to improve the lives of others is working!  We seek success, and we need to know when we’ve attained it!  As we work to improve social conditions that seem intractable, results are often difficult to characterize and track. Results in the field of social change are complex, affected by myriad factors, and not immediately apparent. Achieving results is a long-term process. Yet it is important and increasingly feasible to articulate desired results for improving social conditions.

Informational resources on achieving measurable results are readily available in private sector literature on management, productivity, and profit.  However, the movement toward “evidence-based” practice, results-oriented decision making, and results-based grantmaking is in a relatively nascent stage of development in the public and independent sectors.  Strategies, tools, and mechanisms for articulating and documenting progress toward fulfilling the goals of an initiative are being crafted by those in the nonprofit world who recognize the value of demonstrating how interventions improve outcomes for individuals, communities, and society as a whole.

The T. L. Hill Group facilitates members of nonprofit organizations, philanthropic institutions, and partnerships interested in affecting social change in clarifying desired results for their initiatives and identifying milestones along with strategies for attaining them.

TYPES OF PRODUCTS

All projects undertaken by the T. L. Hill Group are customized.  As a result, all products are tailored to meet the expectations specified in a scope of work negotiated with each client.  No “boiler plate” formats are used.  Therefore, in lieu of product templates, the T. L. Hill Group is able to share the examples below listed of products completed to date.  Any new work would result in a product uniquely designed to meet particular needs of the client.

  • Results Frameworks engage partners from diverse disciplines and sectors in developing strategies and achieving results.
  • Managing by Results involves using a commonly developed frame of reference and accompanying management tools with multiple partners to ensure that every contributor stays focused on the desired outcomes for an initiative.
  • Results-based Decision Making is a process that engages multiple partners in using a logic model to clarify underlying assumptions, articulate the rationale for pursuing particular courses of action, and utilize information - qualitative and quantitative - to determine the optimal course of action when confronted with choices.

CLIENTS who have tapped into this expertise

A select group of projects implemented by the T. L. Hill Group which demonstrate skill in designing and using results frameworks with multiple partners is below listed.
  • Annie E. Casey Foundation – Designed an operational framework and management tools which enabled the Foundation’s Technical Assistance Resource Center (TARC) to measure progress in delivering products and services as part of a multi-year initiative currently underway in several U.S. sites aimed at improving the lives of low-income children and families.  (July through December 2001).
  • Center for the Study of Social Policy (http://www.cssp.org)– Collaborated with staff of the Center for the Study of Social Policy in Washington, D.C. to design a results framework and management tools that were used to identify future opportunities and directions for strategic growth (July 2002 through December 2003).
  • Commonwealth Corporation- Center for Youth Development and Education (http://www.commcorp.org/youth)– Facilitated the design and use of a Literacy Works results framework with the Literacy Works Project Director, Literacy Entrepreneurs, and Literacy Works Project Team members as part of a strategic development project designed to complement fund development activities (February through September 2003). 
  • Irene E. and George A. Davis Foundation (http://www.davisfdn.org)  Step Up Springfield Sustainability Strategy – Facilitated the design and use of an internal framework for partners involved in the Step Up Springfield (http://www.stepupspringfield.org)  initiative in Springfield, Massachusetts, to collectively identify short- and long-term needs and to develop a range of sustainability strategies to address them.
  • National Association of Service and Conservation Corps (http://www.nascc.org) – Facilitated the development of strategic messages using a results framework.  (July 2003 through January 2004).
  • Youth Transition Funders Group (http://www.ytfg.org)– Served as a Co-Founder and facilitated the development of a results framework used by foundation representatives who are members of a philanthropic affinity group aimed at improving outcomes among young adults (January 2002 through May 2003).
