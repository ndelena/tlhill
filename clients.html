Selected Clients 2001-2007

Annie E. Casey Foundation

Designed an operational framework and management tools which enabled the Foundation's Technical Assistance Resource Center (TARC) to measure progress in delivering products and services as part of a multi-year initiative in several U.S. sites aimed at improving the lives of low-income children and families. The operational framework continues to be in use as of 2003 (July through December 2001).

Coordinated a Jobs Initiative strategy under a grant to the Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies aimed at improving labor market outcomes among new and low wage workers by addressing issues of race, ethnicity, and culture in workforce development. Information on this topic may be found on the following website developed as part of this strategy - http://www.aecf.org/jobsandrace. (May 2001 through June 2004).

Center for the Study of Social Policy

Collaborated with staff of the Center and a national advisory group to develop a results framework and management tools that will be used to identify future opportunities and directions for strategic growth. This collaboration resulted in Engaging Youth in Positive, Productive Roles (February 2003), a paper which provides an overview of issues affecting young people 8 to 24 years old and reviews trends in national and state-level youth policy efforts. It offers a beginning framework for a balanced state youth policy agenda and concludes with several specific policy recommendations. It presents a unique approach for applying benchmarks to state youth policy decisions. Both a full report and policy brief are available from the Center for the Study of Social Policy. A description of the report may be found at http://www.cssp.org/publications.

Collaborated with the Center's full staff in Washington, D.C. to design a results framework and management tools for internal use in identifying future opportunities and directions for strategic growth. (July 2002 through December 2003)

Co-facilitated two retreats jointly convened by the Annie E. Casey Foundation, Casey Family Programs, and the Center for the Study of Social Policy to design a collaborative initiative to reduce racial disproportionality and disparities among children of color in the welfare system. (March and September 2004)

Commonwealth Corporation - Center for Youth Development and Education

Facilitated the design and use of a Literacy Works results framework with the Literacy Works Project Director, Literacy Entrepreneurs, and Literacy Works Project Team members as part of a strategic development project designed to complement fund development activities (February through September 2003).

Council on African American Affairs

Conducted a summer program with research fellows to engage them in an environmental scanning project to recommend to the Board of Trustees a strategic direction for the Council's inaugural policy initiative. Recommendations presented by the research fellows were approved by the Ron Brown Scholar Alumni and by the Council on African American Affairs Board of Trustees (June through August 2002).

Jobs for the Future

Produced an internal report submitted to Jobs for the Future entitled, "Cultural Competence in Workforce Development: Integrating Race, Ethnicity & Culture to Advance Labor Market Outcomes," October 2003, which informed the New Futures research project.

Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies

Contributed to the development of a series of tools that examine ways to meet the workforce development needs and interests of community based health facilities. These tools may be found on the following Annie E. Casey Foundation web address:
http://www.aecf.org/publications/data/meeting_tools.pdf

Marguerite Casey Foundation

Produced a report for the Marguerite Casey Foundation's Listening Circles conducted in seven locations across the country with community members, advocates, public agency leaders, families, young people, and others in an unprecedented effort to hear from and integrate the diverse voices of the new foundation's customer base into its grant making plans. (March through July 2002) The report may be found at the following web address: http://www.caseygrants.org/documents/reports/listening_circle_report.pdf

National Association of Service and Conservation Corps

Facilitated NASCC staff members in designing and framing a set of results consistent with NASCC's strategic plan that focus on well-defined target areas and clear messages aimed at positioning NASCC to attract interested partners. (September 2003 through January 2004).

National Youth Employment Coalition

Facilitated the development of an issue area strategy based on a review of the National Youth Employment Coalition's (NYEC) three-year plan and consultation with NYEC staff members with periodic review by the Board. The issue area strategy will be used by NYEC to identify and pursue strategic priorities that reflect the interests of its members and respond to needs and opportunities in the field at-large (February 2002 through September 2003).

U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Center for Mental Health Services

As a subcontractor with the American Institutes for Research, the T. L. Hill Group coordinates efforts of SAMHSA Project Officers and other national partners involved in the Partnerships for Youth Transition Initiative, a four-year cooperative agreement with five sites across the country designed to improve access and quality of mental health services for youth in transition. Information on this project may be found by visiting http://ntacyt.fmhi.usf.edu/partnerships/index.htm (January 2003 to present).

Youth Transition Funders Group

Coordinated and facilitated the development of a results framework that is being used by foundation representatives who are members of a philanthropic affinity group aimed at improving outcomes among young adults (January 2002 through May 2003).
